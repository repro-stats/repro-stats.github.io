reproducr
CRAN

The reproducibility foundation. Audit, certify, and monitor R scripts and environments for regulated settings. Automates 21 CFR Part 11 compliance for your analyses.

  • Script-level audit trail with cryptographic hash
  • Risk scoring for regulatory compliance
  • Environment certification and lockfiles
  • Drift detection across analysis runs
> install.packages("reproducr")
Docs & source →
bayprior
CRAN Shiny app

Structured Bayesian prior elicitation, conflict diagnostics, sensitivity analysis, and regulatory reporting for clinical trials. Aligned with the FDA 2026 draft guidance on Bayesian methods.

  • SHELF-style prior elicitation across 6 distribution families
  • Prior-data conflict diagnostics (Box p-value, Bhattacharyya)
  • Sensitivity analysis with tornado plots and influence heatmaps
  • Regulatory-ready prior justification reports (HTML/PDF/Word)
  • Interactive Shiny application for non-programmers
> install.packages("bayprior")
Docs & source →
regulog
In development

Electronic audit logging for R, built to 21 CFR Part 11 standards. Tamper-evident, user-attributed, timestamp-verified logging of all analytical actions.

  • Immutable audit log with digital signatures
  • User-attributed action tracking with timestamps
  • Structured output for regulatory review
  • Deep integration with reproducr
> remotes::install_github("repro-stats/regulog")
View on GitHub →
lineager
In development

Row-level data provenance for clinical datasets. Track every transformation from raw SDTM input to analysis-ready ADaM datasets with full traceability.

  • Row-level transformation tracking
  • SDTM to ADaM derivation audit trail
  • Visual provenance graph generation
  • CDISC-compatible lineage documentation
> remotes::install_github("repro-stats/lineager")
View on GitHub →
estimandr
In development

ICH E9(R1) estimand framework for R. Define, document, and implement your estimand strategy — population, variable, intercurrent events, summary measure — directly in your analysis pipeline. Pairs naturally with bayprior for Bayesian estimand analyses.

  • Structured estimand specification objects
  • Intercurrent event strategy documentation
  • SAP-ready estimand output tables
  • Integrates with clinical trial analysis packages
> remotes::install_github("repro-stats/estimandr")

About ReproStats

Why these packages
exist.

Pharmaceutical data science has a reproducibility problem. Most clinical analyses cannot be independently reproduced — not because the science was wrong, but because the computational infrastructure around it was never documented properly.

ReproStats is an open-source project building the R infrastructure that makes reproducibility and regulatory compliance the path of least resistance. Five packages covering the full lifecycle: from audit and certification (reproducr, regulog) to Bayesian prior methodology (bayprior), data provenance (lineager), and estimand specification (estimandr).

All packages are peer-reviewed, openly developed on GitHub, and designed from the ground up for GxP environments. reproducr and bayprior are available on CRAN today.

ReproStats is founded by Ndoh Penn, a biostatistician based in Brussels, Belgium. The enterprise platform built on this foundation is Reprosia.
